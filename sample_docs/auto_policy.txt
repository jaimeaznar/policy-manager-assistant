SECTION 1: COVERAGE
================================================================================

1.1 Comprehensive Coverage

This policy provides comprehensive motor insurance covering loss of or damage to your vehicle, liability to third parties, and additional benefits as detailed in this section.

Your vehicle is covered against loss or damage caused by:
- Accidental collision or impact with another vehicle, object, person, or animal
- Fire, explosion, lightning, and self-ignition
- Theft or attempted theft of the vehicle or its parts and accessories
- Vandalism and malicious damage
- Storm, flood, hail, and weight of snow
- Falling objects including trees, branches, and building materials
- Riot, civil commotion, and strikes
- Damage sustained during transit by road, rail, inland waterway, or ferry within Europe

The maximum sum insured for your vehicle is the market value at the time of loss or the agreed value stated on your policy schedule, whichever applies.

1.2 Third-Party Liability

This policy covers your legal liability for:

Bodily injury to third parties: unlimited coverage for death or bodily injury to any person arising from the use of your vehicle, as required by law.

Property damage to third parties: coverage up to EUR 1,200,000 per incident for damage to third-party property caused by the use of your vehicle.

Legal defence costs: reasonable legal costs and expenses incurred with Zurich's written consent in defending any claim covered under this section.

1.3 Personal Accident Cover

This policy includes personal accident coverage for the policyholder and named drivers:
- Death resulting from a vehicle accident: EUR 50,000
- Permanent total disability: EUR 50,000
- Permanent partial disability: percentage of EUR 50,000 according to the disability scale
- Temporary total disability: EUR 75 per day for up to 365 days
- Medical expenses following an accident: EUR 5,000 per person per incident

Personal accident cover applies while travelling in, getting into, or getting out of the insured vehicle, and while carrying out emergency repairs at the roadside.

1.4 Windscreen and Glass Coverage

This policy covers repair or replacement of the windscreen, sunroof, and window glass of your vehicle:
- Windscreen repair: covered in full with no deductible
- Windscreen replacement: EUR 1,500 maximum, subject to EUR 100 deductible
- Side and rear window replacement: EUR 750 per window, subject to EUR 75 deductible

Using a Zurich-approved glass specialist waives the replacement deductible.

1.5 Roadside Assistance and Recovery

24-hour roadside assistance is included with this policy, covering:
- Roadside repair: a technician will attend your location and attempt a repair. If the vehicle cannot be made roadworthy, it will be towed to the nearest approved garage.
- Towing: up to EUR 250 per incident for towing to the nearest approved repair facility.
- Home start: assistance if the vehicle breaks down at or within 1 kilometre of your home address.
- Onward travel: if the vehicle cannot be repaired the same day, Zurich will arrange one of the following: a replacement vehicle for up to 5 days, hotel accommodation for up to 2 nights (EUR 150 per night maximum), or public transport to your original destination.
- European cover: roadside assistance and recovery within all EU member states, Switzerland, Norway, and the United Kingdom, for trips of up to 90 days duration.

1.6 Replacement Vehicle

If your vehicle is stolen or is being repaired following an insured incident, Zurich will provide a replacement vehicle of similar category for up to 14 days. The replacement vehicle is subject to availability and must be collected from and returned to a Zurich-approved location.

If you have selected the enhanced replacement vehicle option, coverage extends to 28 days and includes delivery and collection at your home or workplace.

1.7 Legal Expenses Cover

This policy includes legal expenses coverage of up to EUR 50,000 per incident for:
- Pursuing an uninsured loss recovery claim against a negligent third party (e.g., your deductible, loss of earnings, hire car costs)
- Defending a prosecution arising from a motor accident
- Appealing against licence suspension or penalty points arising from a motor accident

Legal expenses cover is provided subject to a reasonable prospect of success as assessed by Zurich's appointed legal advisors.

SECTION 2: EXCLUSIONS
================================================================================

2.1 General Exclusions

This policy does not cover:

- Loss or damage arising while the vehicle is being driven by a person not named on the policy or not holding a valid driving licence
- Loss or damage while the vehicle is being used for a purpose not permitted under the policy (see Permitted Use)
- Loss or damage while the driver is under the influence of alcohol (blood alcohol level exceeding 0.5 g/L) or drugs not prescribed by a medical practitioner
- Loss or damage caused by driving while disqualified from holding a licence
- Loss or damage caused by the vehicle being used for racing, speed testing, rallying, off-road driving, track days, or any competitive event
- War, invasion, act of foreign enemy, hostilities, civil war, rebellion, revolution, or terrorism
- Nuclear reaction, radiation, or radioactive contamination
- Loss of use, depreciation, wear and tear, mechanical or electrical breakdown not caused by an insured incident
- Damage to tyres caused by braking, punctures, cuts, or bursts unless caused by an accident that also damages other parts of the vehicle
- Confiscation, seizure, or detention by any government, customs, or legal authority

2.2 Theft Exclusions

Theft coverage is excluded:
- If the vehicle was left unlocked, with windows open, or with keys in or on the vehicle
- If the vehicle ignition key or keyless entry device was left unattended in a public place
- Theft by deception (e.g., test drive fraud, phishing scams) unless reported to police within 24 hours
- Theft of personal belongings left in the vehicle (these may be covered under your home contents policy)

2.3 Modification Exclusions

The following modifications are excluded unless declared to and accepted by Zurich:
- Engine modifications increasing power output by more than 10% above the manufacturer's standard specification
- Suspension lowering or lifting kits
- Body kits, spoilers, and non-standard alloy wheels
- Tinted windows beyond factory specification
- Any modification that invalidates the vehicle's type approval or road worthiness certificate

Declared and accepted modifications are covered up to the value stated on the policy schedule.

2.4 Commercial Use Exclusions

This policy does not cover:
- Use of the vehicle for hire or reward (taxi, private hire, ride-sharing for payment)
- Use for the delivery of goods for commercial purposes
- Use as a driving school vehicle
- Carriage of hazardous materials beyond normal domestic quantities

2.5 Territorial Exclusions

Coverage is limited to the following territories:
- All EU member states, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, and the United Kingdom
- Transit through non-covered territories is permitted for journeys between covered territories, provided the transit does not exceed 48 hours

Driving in non-covered territories voids all coverage under this policy for the duration of such use.

SECTION 3: DEDUCTIBLES
================================================================================

3.1 Standard Deductibles

The following deductibles apply to each and every claim:

- Accidental damage: EUR 600 per incident
- Fire and theft: EUR 400 per incident
- Windscreen replacement: EUR 100 per incident (waived if using approved specialist)
- Windscreen repair: EUR 0
- Young driver surcharge (drivers under 25): additional EUR 400 per incident
- Inexperienced driver surcharge (licence held less than 2 years): additional EUR 300 per incident

3.2 Voluntary Excess

You have selected a voluntary excess of EUR [amount] in addition to the standard deductible. This is applied per incident on top of any compulsory or age-related deductibles.

3.3 Non-Fault Claims

If you are involved in an accident that is entirely the fault of an identifiable third party, and Zurich successfully recovers the claim costs from the third party's insurer, your deductible will be refunded in full. Recovery timelines depend on the third party's insurer and typically take 8 to 12 weeks.

3.4 Total Loss Deductible

In the event of a total loss (where repair costs exceed the market value of the vehicle), no deductible is applied. Settlement is based on the market value of the vehicle immediately before the incident, or the agreed value if stated on your policy schedule.

SECTION 4: CLAIMS PROCESS
================================================================================

4.1 What to Do After an Accident

Step 1: Ensure the safety of all persons involved. Call emergency services (112) if anyone is injured.

Step 2: Exchange details with the other party: name, address, vehicle registration, insurance company, and policy number. If there are witnesses, collect their contact details.

Step 3: Take photographs of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, traffic signs, and any relevant details.

Step 4: Do not admit fault or liability at the scene. Do not sign any documents presented by the other party other than the European Accident Statement (constat amiable).

Step 5: Report the accident to Zurich within 48 hours. Call our motor claims line on +41 44 625 25 50 or submit a claim online at www.zurich.com/motorclaims.

Step 6: If the vehicle is not roadworthy, call our roadside assistance number on +41 44 625 25 75 for immediate recovery.

4.2 Making a Claim

To make a claim, provide the following information:
- Your policy number (ZH-AUTO-2024-001)
- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Description of how the incident occurred
- Details of all parties and vehicles involved
- Police report reference number (if applicable)
- Photographs of vehicle damage
- Details of any injuries sustained

A claims handler will be assigned within 1 business day. For driveable vehicles, you will be directed to a Zurich-approved repair centre for assessment. For non-driveable vehicles, an assessor will inspect the vehicle at its current location within 2 business days.

4.3 Repair Process

Zurich operates a network of approved repair centres across Europe. Using an approved repairer provides the following benefits:
- Guaranteed quality of repairs for 3 years
- Direct billing (no upfront payment required from you)
- Replacement vehicle arranged automatically
- Faster claim settlement

You may choose your own repairer, but Zurich reserves the right to have the vehicle inspected before authorising repairs, and the guarantee on repairs will be provided by the repairer directly, not by Zurich.

4.4 Total Loss Settlement

A vehicle is declared a total loss when repair costs exceed the market value of the vehicle. In such cases:
- Zurich will pay the market value of the vehicle at the time of loss, less any outstanding finance
- You retain the option to purchase the salvage at a price determined by Zurich
- Settlement is typically completed within 10 business days of the vehicle being declared a total loss
- GAP insurance (if purchased) covers the difference between the settlement amount and the original purchase price or outstanding finance, whichever is greater

4.5 Windscreen Claims

Windscreen claims can be made directly with our approved glass partner without contacting Zurich first. Call the glass claims line on +41 44 625 25 80. Windscreen repair or replacement does not affect your no claims discount.

SECTION 5: RENEWAL
================================================================================

5.1 Policy Renewal

This policy is an annual contract. Zurich will send a renewal invitation at least 21 days before the expiry date, including:
- Updated premium based on your claims history, driving record, and vehicle age
- Any changes to terms, conditions, or coverage
- Updated market value of the insured vehicle

5.2 Automatic Renewal

Unless you notify us otherwise, this policy will automatically renew on the expiry date. Payment will be taken from your registered payment method. To opt out of automatic renewal, contact us at least 7 days before the expiry date.

5.3 No Claims Discount

Your no claims discount (NCD) is based on consecutive claim-free years:
- 1 year claim-free: 15% discount
- 2 years claim-free: 30% discount
- 3 years claim-free: 40% discount
- 4 years claim-free: 50% discount
- 5 or more years claim-free: 60% discount (maximum)

Windscreen-only claims, roadside assistance calls, and claims where Zurich recovers 100% of costs from a third party do not affect your NCD.

One at-fault claim reduces your NCD by 2 levels. Two or more at-fault claims in a policy year reset your NCD to 0%.

NCD protection is available as an optional add-on. With NCD protection, you are allowed one at-fault claim in any 2-year period without any reduction to your discount.

5.4 Premium Factors

Your renewal premium is calculated based on:
- Claims history and no claims discount level
- Driver age, experience, and licence type
- Vehicle make, model, age, engine size, and insurance group
- Annual mileage declared
- Postcode of the registered keeper's address
- Security features installed on the vehicle
- Voluntary excess selected
- Optional covers included

5.5 Mid-Term Changes

You must notify Zurich within 14 days of any of the following changes:
- Change of vehicle
- Addition or removal of named drivers
- Change of address
- Change of occupation
- Modification to the vehicle
- Change in annual mileage estimate (increase of more than 20%)
- Any driving convictions, penalty points, or fixed penalty notices received by you or any named driver
- Any non-fault or fault claims not yet reported

Failure to notify Zurich of material changes may result in your policy being void and claims being rejected.

5.6 Cancellation

You may cancel this policy at any time. During the 14-day cooling-off period, a full refund is provided (less a pro-rata charge for any days of cover used if a claim has not been made). After the cooling-off period, refunds are calculated on a pro-rata basis less an administration fee of EUR 50. If a claim has been made during the current policy year, no refund is payable.

SECTION 6: GENERAL CONDITIONS
================================================================================

6.1 Permitted Use

Your policy covers the following uses as shown on your policy schedule:
- Social, domestic, and pleasure use (including commuting to a single fixed place of work)
- Business use by the policyholder only (Class 1)
- Business use by the policyholder and named drivers (Class 2)

Use of the vehicle outside the permitted use voids all coverage.

6.2 Vehicle Maintenance

You must maintain the vehicle in a roadworthy condition at all times. This includes:
- Valid MOT certificate (or equivalent national inspection) where required
- Tyres meeting the minimum legal tread depth (1.6mm across the central three-quarters)
- All lights, brakes, and steering in proper working order
- Compliance with all applicable vehicle safety regulations

6.3 Security Requirements

You must take reasonable precautions to protect the vehicle from loss or damage:
- Lock all doors and close all windows when leaving the vehicle unattended
- Activate any fitted alarm, immobiliser, or tracking device
- Do not leave the vehicle keys unattended or in the vehicle
- Park in a secure location where possible, especially overnight

6.4 Fraud

If any claim is found to be fraudulent, or if fraudulent means are used to obtain benefit under this policy, all coverage will be void. Zurich will not pay any fraudulent claim and may recover any payments already made. The policyholder may also be reported to the relevant authorities.

6.5 Governing Law

This policy is governed by the laws of Switzerland. Disputes shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Zurich.
