SECTION 1: COVERAGE
================================================================================

1.1 Buildings Coverage

This policy covers the structure of your home including walls, roof, floors, ceilings, doors, windows, fixtures and fittings, permanent extensions, garages, outbuildings, swimming pools, terraces, patios, driveways, fences, gates and walls forming part of your property.

Buildings coverage includes damage caused by:
- Fire, smoke, explosion, and lightning strike
- Storm, flood, and weight of snow
- Escape of water from fixed water systems, tanks, apparatus or pipes
- Escape of oil from fixed heating installations
- Theft or attempted theft involving forced entry
- Collision by vehicles, aircraft, or animals
- Falling trees, telegraph poles, lamp posts, or satellite dishes
- Subsidence, heave, or landslip of the site on which the buildings stand
- Vandalism and malicious damage

The maximum sum insured for buildings coverage under this policy is EUR 500,000.

1.2 Contents Coverage

This policy covers household goods, personal belongings, furniture, furnishings, clothing, appliances, electronic equipment, and other items owned by you or your family members permanently residing at the insured property.

Contents coverage includes:
- All items within the main dwelling
- Items in garages, outbuildings, and sheds on the property
- Items temporarily removed from the home (up to 60 days, worldwide)
- Refrigerated and frozen food (up to EUR 1,000) following mechanical breakdown or power failure
- Contents in the open within the boundaries of the property (up to EUR 2,500)

The maximum sum insured for contents coverage under this policy is EUR 75,000.

High-value items (individual items worth more than EUR 2,500) must be listed separately on the policy schedule. Unlisted high-value items are covered up to a maximum of EUR 2,500 per item.

1.3 Personal Valuables Coverage

Personal valuables including jewellery, watches, fine art, antiques, collections, musical instruments, cameras, laptops, tablets, and mobile phones are covered both inside and outside the home, subject to the following limits:

- Single item limit: EUR 5,000 (unless specified on the policy schedule)
- Total personal valuables limit: EUR 15,000
- Unattended vehicle limit: EUR 1,500 (items must be in a locked boot or concealed compartment)

1.4 Alternative Accommodation

If the insured property becomes uninhabitable due to an insured event, this policy covers reasonable costs of alternative accommodation for you and your household, including:
- Hotel or rental accommodation costs
- Storage of furniture and belongings
- Additional travel costs to school or workplace

The maximum period of alternative accommodation is 24 months. The maximum sum insured for alternative accommodation is EUR 50,000.

1.5 Liability Coverage

This policy includes personal liability coverage for you and members of your household for legal liability arising from accidental bodily injury to third parties or accidental damage to third-party property occurring anywhere in the world.

Personal liability sum insured: EUR 2,000,000 per occurrence.

This includes liability arising from:
- Ownership or occupation of the insured property
- Personal activities of household members
- Domestic employees engaged by you
- Ownership of domestic pets (excluding dogs classified under dangerous breeds legislation)

SECTION 2: EXCLUSIONS
================================================================================

2.1 General Exclusions

This policy does not cover loss or damage caused by or resulting from:

- War, invasion, act of foreign enemy, hostilities, civil war, rebellion, revolution, insurrection, military or usurped power, or confiscation by order of any government or public authority
- Nuclear reaction, nuclear radiation, or radioactive contamination
- Terrorism, except where Zurich has opted to provide terrorism coverage under a government-backed scheme
- Intentional or deliberate acts by you, your family, or anyone acting with your knowledge or consent
- Wear and tear, gradual deterioration, rust, corrosion, mould, mildew, damp, wet or dry rot, woodworm, or vermin
- Mechanical or electrical breakdown of domestic appliances unless caused by an insured event
- Any process of cleaning, dyeing, repair, alteration, or restoration
- Confiscation or detention by customs or other officials
- Any loss, damage, or liability arising from your trade, business, or profession conducted at the insured property

2.2 Flood Exclusions

Damage caused by flooding is excluded if:
- The property is located in a designated high-risk flood zone (Flood Zone 3) and additional flood cover has not been purchased
- The flooding results from failure to maintain drains, gutters, or watercourses for which you are responsible
- The flooding is caused by gradual seepage or rising damp over a period exceeding 14 days

2.3 Subsidence Exclusions

Subsidence, heave, or landslip damage is excluded for:
- Damage to driveways, paths, patios, swimming pools, tennis courts, fences, gates, or walls unless the main dwelling is also affected
- Damage caused by new structures or extensions built within the last 12 months
- Damage resulting from defective design, materials, or workmanship
- Normal settlement or bedding down of new structures
- Coastal or river erosion
- Movement caused by the action of tree roots where the trees were planted after the start of this policy and within 10 metres of the main dwelling

2.4 Water Damage Exclusions

Escape of water damage is excluded:
- If the property has been left unoccupied for more than 60 consecutive days unless the water supply has been turned off at the mains and the system drained
- For damage caused by gradual leaks that have been present for more than 30 days
- For the cost of replacing or repairing the appliance, pipe, or tank from which water has escaped (the resulting damage to other property is covered)

2.5 Theft Exclusions

Theft or attempted theft is excluded:
- If the property has been left unoccupied for more than 60 consecutive days
- Where there is no evidence of forcible and violent entry or exit (unless theft is committed by someone lawfully in the property)
- Theft from outbuildings unless there was forced and violent entry
- Theft of items from motor vehicles unless all windows and doors were closed and locked, and the item was in a concealed compartment or locked boot

- Buildings claims: EUR 500 per incident

- Escape of water claims: EUR 750 per incident

3.2 Voluntary Excess

You have selected a voluntary excess of EUR [amount] in addition to the standard deductible. This reduces your annual premium by the percentage shown on your policy schedule.

The total deductible for any claim is the standard deductible plus your voluntary excess.

3.3 Deductible Waiver

The deductible is waived (reduced to EUR 0) in the following circumstances:

- Claims arising from a declared natural disaster (as defined by the relevant government authority)
- Liability claims where Zurich has agreed to handle the claim on your behalf

SECTION 4: CLAIMS PROCESS
================================================================================

4.1 How to Make a Claim

To make a claim under this policy, follow these steps:

Step 1: Contact us as soon as possible and no later than 30 days after the incident. Call our 24-hour claims line on +41 44 625 25 25 or submit a claim online at www.zurich.com/claims.

Step 2: Provide your policy number (ZH-HOME-2024-001) and a detailed description of the incident, including the date, time, and circumstances.

Step 3: For theft or vandalism claims, report the incident to the police within 24 hours and obtain a crime reference number. Provide this number with your claim.

Step 4: Take reasonable steps to prevent further damage. For example, if a pipe has burst, turn off the water supply at the mains. Do not dispose of damaged items until an assessor has inspected them.

Step 5: Provide supporting evidence including photographs, receipts, valuations, repair estimates, and any other documentation relevant to your claim.

Step 6: A claims handler will be assigned to your case within 2 business days. For emergency claims (escape of water, fire damage, storm damage making the property insecure), we will arrange an emergency response within 4 hours.

4.2 Claims Settlement

Buildings claims are settled on a reinstatement basis, meaning we pay the cost of repairing or rebuilding the damaged structure to its condition immediately before the loss, using materials of similar quality.

Contents claims are settled on a new-for-old basis for items less than 5 years old. Items older than 5 years are settled on an indemnity basis, meaning the current market value is paid, taking into account wear and tear and depreciation.

Personal valuables are settled based on the current replacement cost or the sum insured on the policy schedule, whichever is lower.

We aim to settle straightforward claims within 10 business days of receiving all required documentation. Complex claims involving subsidence, large fires, or disputed liability may take longer.

4.3 Emergency Assistance

Our 24-hour emergency helpline provides immediate assistance for:
- Burst pipes and escape of water: emergency plumber dispatch
- Fire damage: emergency boarding up and making safe
- Break-in: emergency locksmith and temporary security
- Storm damage: emergency roof repairs and tarpaulin coverage

Emergency repairs up to EUR 1,500 per incident are authorised without prior approval from a claims handler.

SECTION 5: RENEWAL
================================================================================

5.1 Policy Renewal

This policy is an annual contract. We will contact you at least 30 days before the expiry date with a renewal offer, including:
- Updated premium for the next policy year
- Any changes to terms, conditions, or coverage
- Updated rebuild cost assessment for buildings coverage

5.2 Automatic Renewal

Unless you instruct us otherwise, this policy will automatically renew on the expiry date. Your registered payment method will be charged the renewal premium. You may opt out of automatic renewal at any time by contacting us or updating your preferences online at www.zurich.com/myaccount.

5.3 Premium Adjustments

Your renewal premium may be adjusted based on:
- Claims history during the current policy year
- Changes in the rebuild cost index for your area
- Changes in risk factors (e.g., local flood risk reclassification)
- Changes in Insurance Premium Tax rates

5.4 No Claims Discount

This policy includes a no claims discount (NCD) scheme:
- 1 claim-free year: 10% discount
- 2 consecutive claim-free years: 20% discount
- 3 consecutive claim-free years: 30% discount
- 5 or more consecutive claim-free years: 40% discount (maximum)

One claim in a policy year reduces your NCD by one level. Two or more claims in a policy year reset your NCD to 0%.

NCD protection is available as an optional add-on. With NCD protection, your first claim in any 3-year period does not affect your discount level.

5.5 Mid-Term Adjustments

You may request changes to your policy at any time during the policy year, including:
- Increasing or decreasing coverage limits
- Adding or removing listed high-value items
- Adding optional covers (e.g., accidental damage, home emergency)
- Updating your property details (e.g., after renovations)

Mid-term adjustments may result in an additional premium charge or a partial refund, calculated on a pro-rata basis for the remaining policy term.

5.6 Cancellation

You may cancel this policy at any time by giving us 14 days' written notice. If you cancel within the first 14 days of the policy (cooling-off period), you will receive a full refund of any premium paid, provided no claims have been made. After the cooling-off period, a refund will be calculated on a pro-rata basis, less an administration fee of EUR 25.

SECTION 6: GENERAL CONDITIONS
================================================================================

6.1 Duty of Care

You must take reasonable precautions to prevent loss, damage, or injury, including:
- Keeping the property in good repair and condition
- Securing all doors and windows when the property is unoccupied
- Maintaining fire detection and security alarm systems in working order
- Not leaving the property unoccupied for more than 60 consecutive days without notifying Zurich

6.2 Disclosure

You must inform Zurich of any material changes to the information provided when this policy was taken out, including changes to:
- The construction of the property (e.g., flat roof extensions)
- The use of the property (e.g., letting to tenants, running a business)
- The number of occupants
- Any criminal convictions of household members
- Previous claims history not previously disclosed

6.3 Other Insurance

If any loss or damage covered by this policy is also covered by another insurance policy, Zurich will only pay its proportionate share of the claim.

6.4 Fraud

If any claim is found to be fraudulent in any respect, all coverage under this policy will be void. Zurich reserves the right to recover any payments already made and to cancel the policy without refund.

6.5 Governing Law

This policy is governed by and shall be construed in accordance with the laws of Switzerland. Any disputes shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Zurich.
